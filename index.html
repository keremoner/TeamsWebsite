Istanbul

Istanbul, a captivating city which lies between Europe and Asia has been a center of culture, trade and tourism for centuries. As well as being the biggest city in Turkey, Istanbul is also one of the most populated cities in the world with approximately 15 million citizens. According to the ex-prime minister Yıldırım, because of the high population density, the most crucial problem in Istanbul is traffic congestion.

Traffic Congestion

According to INRIX 2018 Traffic Scorecard Report, Istanbul is considered as the second most congested city in the world, and drivers in Istanbul spent 157 hours in congestion on average.

Car Accidents

Istanbul has the highest rate of car accident in Turkey in the first six month of 2015. According to the TomTom Traffic Index, total of 129 people died in 7,422 accidents. 89.6 percent of faults that caused these accidents were drivers’ faults.

Inability to Drive of Disabled People

People with physical and mental disorders such as epilepsy, myopathy, encephalitis lethargica patients are not allowed to drive unless a doctor report. However, they have the transportation right and need as every other individual.

High Stress

As a result of a conference organised by the Company EasyJet, Istanbul is considered as one of the most stressful metropoles. Traffic related stress decreases the life quality of the citizens.

Air Pollution

According to the Turkey’s Chamber of Environmental Engineers’ Air Pollution Report 2017, it is stated that traffic congestion in the city causes air pollution.

Noise

According to the World Economic Forum’s website, Istanbul is considered as the worst fifth city in the world in terms of noise pollution.
